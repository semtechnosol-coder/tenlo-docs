# Quick start.

Eight steps, in order. Each one unlocks the next item on your dashboard checklist. Total: about 25 minutes of clicking, plus whatever time you spend on brand voice.

<Steps>
<Step n={1} title="Add your business mailing address." time="~2 min" path="Settings → Business contact info">

Open **Settings**, find the *Business mailing address* card, paste your full address — street, suite, city, province, postal code, country — and click **Save changes**. A "Mailing address saved ✓" toast confirms it's done.

<Callout type="legal" title="Why this comes first · CASL §6(2)">
Canadian anti-spam law requires the sender's physical address on every commercial email and SMS. Tenlo enforces this in code — sends are blocked until the address is saved. Do it now so you don't hit a wall at Step 5.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step n={2} title="Connect Google Business Profile." time="~3 min" path="Settings → Google Business Profile">

Click **Connect Google Business Profile**, sign in with the Google account that *owns* the listing, and approve the permission. You'll return to the dashboard and see <Pill v="replied">connected</Pill>. Existing reviews import in 1–2 minutes.

<Callout type="warn" title="Use the owner account">
A customer Google account won't carry the `business.manage` permission Tenlo needs. The connection either fails or attaches to the wrong listing — and is a pain to undo.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step n={3} title="Teach the AI how you sound." time="~5 min" path="Settings → Brand voice">

Two fields:

- **Tone description** — 1–2 sentences on how you'd want a reply to read. Direct, warm, technical, conversational — be specific.
- **Sign-off** — how every reply ends. *"— The [Business] Team"* or just your first name.

Or click **Analyse my reviews** to let Tenlo read your existing replies and draft a tone description for you to edit.

<Callout type="tip">
Human-edited replies feed the brand-voice analyser first. The more you tweak AI drafts before approving, the faster Tenlo learns to sound like you.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step n={4} title="Create a message template." time="~5 min" path="Campaigns → Templates">

Go to **Campaigns → Templates → New template**. Write your review-request message. Use `{{contact.first_name}}` to personalise the opening. Tenlo will lint the template on save and flag any missing required tokens.

See [Message templates & A/B testing](/reputation-ai/using-the-product/templates) for the full token reference.
</Step>

<Step n={5} title="Create a campaign." time="~5 min" path="Campaigns → New campaign">

Click **New campaign**, give it a name, choose your template, and set a trigger:

- **Manual** — you fire it when you're ready.
- **Scheduled** — runs at a fixed time (e.g. every Friday at 10am).
- **Webhook** — fires when your CRM or booking system sends a signal.

See [Campaigns](/reputation-ai/using-the-product/campaigns) for full trigger documentation.
</Step>

<Step n={6} title="Send a test review request." time="~2 min" path="Campaigns → your campaign → Test send">

Add your own email or phone number as a contact, click **Send test**, and verify the message arrives and looks right. Check that the review link opens the correct Google listing.
</Step>

<Step n={7} title="Set up auto-response rules." time="~3 min" path="Settings → Routing rules">

Decide which reviews auto-post and which come to you for approval. The recommended starting point:

| Rating | Default action |
|---|---|
| 5 stars | Auto-post after 24h |
| 4 stars | Auto-post after 24h |
| 3 stars | Human queue |
| 1–2 stars | Human queue (always) |

Adjust the **Auto-post delay** if you want a window to catch anything before it goes live.
</Step>

<Step n={8} title="Go live." time="1 min" path="Campaigns → your campaign">

Toggle your campaign from **Draft** to **Active**. The dashboard checklist ticks to complete. You're live.

<Callout type="note">
Reviews left after your connection are fetched once daily at 09:00 UTC. A review posted at 10:00 AM may not appear in your inbox until the following morning.
</Callout>
</Step>
</Steps>
