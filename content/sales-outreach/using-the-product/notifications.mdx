# Notifications.

The system fires alerts on key Sales Outreach events. You decide where they land.

## The events

| Event | Fires when |
|---|---|
| `p02.usage_warning` (80%) | You hit 80% of your monthly approval cap |
| `p02.usage_warning` (95%) | You hit 95% — *"almost at cap"* |
| `p02.cap_reached` | You're at 100% — approval blocked until rollover |

## Adding a channel

**Settings → Notifications → + Add channel.** Pick:

- **Slack** — paste an Incoming Webhook URL (Slack → Apps → Incoming Webhooks → Add to channel)
- **Microsoft Teams** — paste an Incoming Webhook URL (Teams channel → Connectors → Incoming Webhook)
- **Email** — enter an email address

Click **Send test** to verify the wiring before relying on it.

## Subscribing to events

Once a channel is connected, you'll see an event-toggle grid. Each row is an event; each column is one of your channels. Toggle on what you want where.

<Callout type="note" title="Default behaviour when no channels are configured">
If you have **no channels configured**, the original 80%/95% email goes to your account owner email by default — same behaviour as before notifications existed.
</Callout>

## Slack and Teams formatting

- **Slack** uses Block Kit with a coloured stripe (green/amber/red based on the cap level)
- **Teams** uses MessageCard format with similar colour coding

## Auto-revoke

If a webhook URL becomes invalid (channel deleted, app removed, returns 404/410), the system auto-marks it inactive and sends you an email. Re-add it to restore.
