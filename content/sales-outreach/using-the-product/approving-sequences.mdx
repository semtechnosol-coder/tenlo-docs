# Approving & sending.

Once a sequence is in `pending_approval` status (research and generation complete), you click **Approve & send** on the row.

## What happens on approve

1. **Cap check** — the system atomically checks if you have approval cap remaining. If you're at 500/500, the click returns *"Cap reached — contact us for dedicated-mailbox setup"* and the sequence stays pending.
2. **Cap consumed** — your usage counter ticks +1.
3. **Warning fires** if you crossed the 80% or 95% threshold (one-time per period — see [Notifications](/sales-outreach/using-the-product/notifications)).
4. **Step 1 queues** — sends within minutes (subject to your daily 50-email cap).
5. **Steps 2–4 queue** with their respective delays.

## Pause, Resume, Archive

These live in the **row menu** (three-dot menu) on each sequence row.

| Action | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Pause** | Stops further sends. Already-sent emails remain. Status flips to `paused`. |
| **Resume** | Restarts sending. Pending steps fire on their original schedule (or right away if their delay has already passed). |
| **Archive** | **Permanent.** Stops all remaining sends. Sequence becomes read-only. The dashboard asks you to confirm with explicit wording: *"This stops all remaining sends and cannot be undone."* |

<Callout type="note" title="Why pause/resume isn't on the row directly">
Approving is the most common positive action — kept inline, one click. Pausing/archiving are destructive — moved to the row menu so you don't fat-finger them.
</Callout>

## Cap at 100% — what then?

Approval is blocked. You have three options:

- Wait for monthly rollover
- Email hello@tenloai.com to upgrade to a **dedicated mailbox**
- Archive sequences you don't intend to use (doesn't refund the cap, but reduces visual clutter)

See [Pricing, caps & billing](/sales-outreach/reference/billing) for the difference between **approval cap** (500/mo), **import cap** (750/mo), and **daily send cap** (50/day).
