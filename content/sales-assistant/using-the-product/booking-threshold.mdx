# The booking threshold.

**Settings → Widget configuration → Booking score threshold**. A slider from 30 to 90, default 60.

## What it controls

Every captured lead gets a 0–100 score. The bot offers a Calendly link only when it judges the lead would likely score at or above your threshold. Below threshold, the bot collects contact info but doesn't push for a meeting.

The bot estimates this judgment in real time during the chat. A separate post-conversation scoring pass produces the official score you see in Recent Leads — that score is computed independently and may differ from the AI's in-conversation estimate.

<Callout type="note" title="No-Calendly caveat">
The threshold only gates the Calendly booking card. If you haven't connected Calendly, moving the slider has no functional effect — the bot will still make its own "good fit" judgment and offer the next step described in your [Products & services field](/sales-assistant/using-the-product/greeting-and-tone), and the slider's value is ignored.
</Callout>

## Threshold guide

| Threshold | Behaviour |
|---|---|
| 30–40 | Permissive — almost every lead gets a booking offer. Use if your Calendly is empty and you want volume to build judgement. |
| 50 | Loose — most leads get offered. Catches some weak fits. |
| 60 *(default)* | Balanced — solid SQLs get the offer; questionable leads don't. |
| 70 | Strict — only clearly-qualified leads. Better quality, fewer bookings. |
| 80–90 | Very strict — only no-doubt fits. Use if your team's calendar is precious. |

## Status labels are independent of the threshold

The Recent Leads card tags each lead with a status badge — 🔥 Hot for scores ≥ 80, ✅ Good fit for ≥ 60, 📋 Low fit for < 60. These cut-offs are **hard-coded; they don't move with your slider**.

So if you set the threshold to 80, leads scoring 65 will still be labeled ✅ Good fit in the dashboard but won't receive a booking offer. The threshold tunes booking *offers*; the dashboard tags reflect raw qualification *quality*.

## Tuning advice

Start at 60. After 10–20 leads, look at the scores in Recent Leads:

- If most of your good-fit leads scored 50–65, drop the threshold to 55.
- If borderline leads are scoring 70+ and getting unwanted offers, raise to 65.

## Threshold and ICP work together

**ICP** = who counts as a good fit. **Threshold** = how strict the bot is when calling someone "qualified."

A clear ICP makes the threshold work better. Don't try to tune the threshold without first writing a real ICP — see [Defining your Ideal Customer](/sales-assistant/using-the-product/icp).
