# Quick start.

Six steps, in order. The Getting Started checklist on your dashboard at [app.tenloai.com/dashboard/assistant](https://app.tenloai.com/dashboard/assistant) mirrors these steps and ticks them off automatically once Tenlo detects you've done them.

<Steps>
<Step n={1} title="Configure your ideal customer." time="~10 min" path="Settings → Widget configuration → Ideal Customer Profile">

Describe in plain language who is a good fit and who isn't. Two paragraphs is fine. Include the industries you serve, company size, geography, decision-maker profile, and — critically — your explicit disqualifiers.

In the same card, add your **Qualifying questions** (one per line). Examples:

- What's your current process for X?
- How big is your team?
- What's your timeline?
- Have you tried solutions like ours before?

<Callout type="tip" title="The single most important field">
ICP drives everything downstream — scoring quality, graceful exits from bad-fit visitors, even the AI's choice of qualifying questions per visitor. Don't skip this. See [Defining your Ideal Customer](/sales-assistant/using-the-product/icp) for examples.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step n={2} title="Set your greeting and tone." time="~3 min" path="Settings → Widget configuration">

**Opening greeting** — the first message visitors see. Keep it warm, specific, and curious. Avoid "How can I help you?" — too open-ended.

> "Hi! I'm here to help you figure out if Acme is the right fit. What brings you in today?"

**Tone description** — one sentence describing how the bot should sound:

> "Friendly, professional, and concise."

The AI uses this verbatim as a style guide. See [Greeting, tone & qualifying questions](/sales-assistant/using-the-product/greeting-and-tone).
</Step>

<Step n={3} title="Connect Calendly and set your booking threshold." time="~5 min" path="Settings → Widget configuration → Connect Calendly">

Click **Connect Calendly** → OAuth flow → pick your event type (e.g. "30-min Discovery Call"). The bot will offer this link to qualified visitors.

In the same card, the **Booking score threshold** slider (range 30–90, default 60) controls how qualified a visitor must be before the bot offers them the booking link. Higher = pickier. See [The booking threshold](/sales-assistant/using-the-product/booking-threshold).

<Callout type="note">
If you skip Calendly, the bot still qualifies leads — it just won't offer a booking link. It will point qualified visitors to whatever next step you describe in your **Products & services** field. See [Calendly](/sales-assistant/using-the-product/calendly).
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step n={4} title="Copy the embed code." time="~1 min" path="Settings → Embed your widget">

Copy the `<script>` snippet from the **Embed your widget** card. The snippet contains your `data-business-id` and — if you changed the widget colour — your `data-primary-color` attribute.
</Step>

<Step n={5} title="Install on your website." time="~5 min">

Paste the snippet just before `</body>` on every page where you want the chat bubble. The dashboard's Getting Started checklist has platform tabs for WordPress, Squarespace, Wix, Shopify, and custom HTML.

<Callout type="warn" title="Webflow customers">
The dashboard's per-platform helper doesn't include a Webflow tab — paste the snippet at **Project Settings → Custom Code → Footer Code** instead.
</Callout>

See [Embedding the widget](/sales-assistant/using-the-product/embed) for the full per-platform table.
</Step>

<Step n={6} title="Wait for your first lead." time="auto" path="Recent Leads tab">

This step completes automatically when the first qualified visitor leaves their contact info.

To test sooner, open your site in an incognito tab, click the bubble, and have a real conversation — the lead should appear in **Recent Leads** within seconds.
</Step>
</Steps>

That's it — your Sales Assistant is live, running 24/7.
